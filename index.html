Stand up for Somalia

Christine Kolesnikov

Hypnosis - Senior Editor

People are dying of hunger... there’s no water’

In Somalia, more than 6 million people are in need of food assistance – more than half the population. But even inside the camps, food is scarce.

Let’s help them. I know you can

Girls, boys and families are experiencing food insecurity, higher food and other commodity prices coupled with a decrease in remittances, strained public services and significant protection challenges.

UNICEF appeals for US$177 million to provide humanitarian services to 1.3 million people, including 826,000 children in Somalia.

Still ‘neglected’

“The situation is critical in Somalia. People are dying of hunger and there is no water,” said Mogadishu resident Noor Ibrahim, who fled his home to seek help at a camp.

“Al-Shabaab blocks the roads, there is no access for food aid, the Shabaab steal food as well.

We didn’t eat this morning, we haven’t had a meal in ten days. We are still waiting for food.

The past days have been cruel on Fatumata and her two young children – forced to walk over 100 miles in search of food, water and sanctuary.

Her three-year-old daughter Shankaron sits on her lap, eyes blank and scared. It is a look that is far too common in these parts.
